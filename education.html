  • 11-485: Introduction to Deep Learning ðŸ©·ðŸŒŸ

    • Difficulty: 4/5

    Overall: The professor is amazing. She teaches content straight out of the textbook she wrote which means she knows it super well, and classes are structured and paced perfectly.   • 21-241: Linear Transformations and Matrices ðŸ©·

    • Difficulty: 4/5

    Overall: A lot of this class is just letting your brain marinate on the content you've injested, but it's very rewarding and you can definitely feel your brain grow as the class progresses. Content was super interesting and dealt with many abstract topics. This was definitely the hardest class of my freshman year but the TAs and professors are super accommodating, helpful, and intelligent.

    Exams: Very difficult for me. Some questions would have multiple versions of different difficulties and point values; I would suggest thinking through the lower point value ones first and sketch a solution before doing the full point questions unless you're very confident in your answer.

    Homeworks: Difficult but fun. You write up the solutions in a timed, proctored environment called a writing session, but the questions are posted ahead of time so really you have a lot of time to think. You will surely become very close to the group you choose, and other people in the class, as you guys struggle together.

Fall 2024

  • 21-127: Concepts in Mathematics ðŸ©·

    • Difficulty: 4/5

    I LOVED THIS CLASS. WHOLEHEARTEDLY. Professor (John Mackey) is genuinely the best lecturer and an incredibly interesting person. The content (discrete math at other universities plus some extra fun stuff) was also transformative and formalized everything I knew about CS, problem solving, and technical communication. Also served as an introduction to LaTeX which I love almost as much as I loved this class. Met some of my favorite people in this class, and also bonded with others later on over our love for 15-151.     Homeworks were so fun. I loved LaTeXing it all up and creating beauty from mathematics. There were also optional bonus problems that had some interesting tricks to them.
